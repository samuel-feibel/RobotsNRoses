Robots 'N' Roses

Adding Hardware

We added 2 light sensors in the front of the robot to detect if the robot has veered off the line and to detect intersections. We placed these about 2 inches apart in order to straddle the line. In addition we swapped out our oversized wheels for smaller, more stable ones. This improved the consistency of our motion but did mean we had to retune the cutoff value between black and white for the light sensor.

Line Following

Figure 1: Line following code

While the robot is moving forward, we poll each light sensor. If the right sensor detects the line, the robot is veering to the left, so the robot turns right to center itself with respects to the line (vice versa for the left sensor).

Square Following

Figure 2: Square following code

As an intermediate step we had the robot take all left turns in a square. These turns, that we called auto_left and auto_right, use the following logic. The robot turns for a finite amount of time in order for the outside sensor to pass over the current white line and white line opposite its motion. Then it continues to turn until the outside sensor hits the white line, signalling that it is again straddling the line. This method was slightly difficult to tune and implement; for the future, 3 sensors may be easier.

Figure 8/h1>

Figure 2: Figure 8 code

When our light sensors detect an intersection (both light sensors detect a white line), our robot turns left or right then continues to follow the line until the next intersection. To traverse a figure 8, our robot turns right at the first intersection, left for 4 intersections, and right for 2 intersections. At the eighth intersection, our robot stops moving.
